01  /  Concept exploration

Four directions. One carried the conviction.

Chosen
Direction A
The Flame
Tongues of fire at Pentecost; “fervent in spirit” (Rom 12:11). The Spirit’s work in the disciple.
Direction B
The Way
The earliest name for followers — “people of the Way.” A threshold, an opening to walk through.
Direction C
The Seed
The parable of the sower. Formation as growth — a seed split open by a single emerging shoot.
μ
Direction D
The Letter
The Greek μ of μαθητής — letterform as mark. Literary, quiet, unmistakably rooted in the name.
02  /  The mark

A single flame — sculpted, leaning, alive.

One continuous shape, never a composition. The tip leans right the way a real flame curls; the inner notch keeps it from reading as a teardrop. Optically centered — sat a touch low so it feels anchored, not floating.
